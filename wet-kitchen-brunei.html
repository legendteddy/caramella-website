Why Wet Kitchens Fail in Brunei

Wet kitchens face conditions that dry kitchens don't:

Direct Water Exposure

Splashes from washing fish, vegetables, and cookware. Water runs down cabinet faces and pools at base cabinets.

Steam and Heat

Constant cooking generates humidity spikes. Steam condenses on cabinet surfaces and interior panels.

Limited Ventilation

Many wet kitchens have windows instead of AC. Moisture lingers, especially in base cabinets near the floor.

Edge Exposure

Cut edges on poorly sealed boards absorb moisture like a sponge. Swelling starts at edges and spreads.

Result: Swollen cabinet doors, peeling laminate edges, drawer boxes that jam, and soft spots in base panels within 2-3 years if materials and detailing aren't specified for the environment.

How Caramella Builds Wet Kitchen Cabinets

1. Moisture-Resistant Board Selection

We use melamine-faced boards (not raw MDF or particleboard) with low formaldehyde emission (ENF grade where specified). The factory-applied melamine layer provides a moisture barrier on face surfaces.

For extreme wet areas (under-sink cabinets, outdoor kitchens), we recommend marine-grade plywood or aluminum carcasses.

2. EVA Edge Sealing at 190°C

Every exposed edge is sealed with EVA (Ethylene-vinyl acetate) hot-melt adhesive applied at 190°C using an automatic edge banding machine. This creates a tight, waterproof bond between the edge tape and the board.

Why it matters: PVC edge tape applied with weak adhesive peels off when exposed to steam and water. EVA bonding resists heat, moisture, and cleaning chemicals.

3. Sealed Drawer Boxes

Drawer boxes are constructed from 18mm boards with pocket-screw joints and edge-sealed on all exposed sides. This prevents moisture ingress from daily use.

4. Base Cabinet Detailing

  • Adjustable legs (100mm minimum) — lifts the cabinet off the floor to prevent direct water contact and allow airflow
  • Waterproof kickboards — PVC or aluminum, not MDF wrapped in vinyl
  • Drip edges on countertops — prevents water runoff from soaking into cabinet face frames

5. Hinge and Hardware Protection

We use European hinges with nickel-plated or stainless steel finish (Blum or equivalent). Drawer slides are specified for damp environments (DTC or Blum soft-close runners with corrosion-resistant coating).

Common Wet Kitchen Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Raw MDF or Particleboard

Problem: Absorbs moisture like a sponge. Swells, warps, and loses structural integrity.

Fix: Use melamine-faced boards or marine plywood for all panels exposed to moisture.

❌ Weak Edge Tape

Problem: PVC edge tape applied with low-temperature adhesive peels off in steam and heat.

Fix: EVA edge sealing applied at 190°C creates a waterproof bond that resists heat and moisture.

❌ Cabinets Sitting on Floor

Problem: Water pools under base cabinets. Kickboards and cabinet bottoms absorb moisture, leading to rot and mold.

Fix: Use adjustable plastic legs (≥100mm) to lift cabinets off the floor and allow airflow.

❌ Cheap Hardware

Problem: Basic steel hinges and runners rust in humid environments. Doors sag, drawers jam.

Fix: Nickel-plated or stainless steel hinges and corrosion-resistant drawer slides (Blum, DTC).

Wet Kitchen Design Considerations

Ventilation

Proper ventilation is critical. If your wet kitchen doesn't have a window or exhaust fan, moisture accumulates inside cabinets and causes damage over time.

Recommendation: Install an exhaust fan or range hood that vents to the outside (not recirculating). For enclosed wet kitchens, consider a ventilation grille above the door.

Drainage

Ensure your floor has a slight slope toward the floor drain. Water should flow away from cabinets, not pool at the cabinet base.

Lighting

Wet kitchens often have poor natural light. LED strip lighting under wall cabinets improves visibility and reduces moisture-related shadows where mold can grow unnoticed.

Storage Layout

Store frequently used items (oils, seasonings, utensils) in wall cabinets or drawers, not in base cabinets near the floor where humidity is highest.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use the same materials for wet kitchen and dry kitchen?

You can, but it's not optimal. Wet kitchens need more robust moisture protection: better edge sealing, corrosion-resistant hardware, and elevated base cabinets. Dry kitchens can use lighter-duty specs since they don't face direct water exposure.

How long does a well-built wet kitchen last in Brunei?

With proper materials (melamine-faced boards, EVA edge sealing, corrosion-resistant hardware) and regular cleaning, 10-15+ years. Poorly built wet kitchens show swelling and peeling within 2-3 years.

Do I need marine plywood for a standard wet kitchen?

Not necessarily. For most enclosed wet kitchens with basic window ventilation, melamine-faced boards with proper EVA edge sealing are sufficient. Marine plywood is recommended for outdoor kitchens, under-sink cabinets, or semi-open areas exposed to rain.

What's the best countertop material for wet kitchens?

Quartz and granite are ideal — non-porous, heat-resistant, and easy to clean. Solid surface (e.g., Corian-type) is also good but can scratch more easily. Avoid laminate countertops in wet kitchens; the seams can absorb water over time.

Can Caramella build outdoor wet kitchens?

Yes. We specify marine-grade plywood or aluminum carcasses, stainless steel hardware, and weather-resistant finishes for outdoor installations. Pricing starts higher due to material upgrades and drainage planning.

How do I maintain my wet kitchen cabinets?

1) Wipe down cabinet faces after heavy cooking to remove moisture and grease. 2) Keep base cabinet doors open occasionally to air out interiors. 3) Check under-sink for leaks monthly. 4) Avoid soaking cabinet surfaces when mopping floors.
