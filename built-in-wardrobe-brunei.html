The Engineering Truth

The Brunei Closet Crisis (Mold & Leather)

Most imported or lower-cost wardrobes in Brunei use untreated LPL (Melamine) cores. In a closed bedroom with 80%–90% relative humidity, these boards become breeding grounds for mold spores. If you've ever found "white powder" on your leather bags or a damp smell on your silk shirts, your wardrobe has failed its primary job.

Our solution is Humidity Isolation. We use ENF-grade moisture-resistant plywood carcasses surfaced with High-Pressure Laminate (HPL) on all faces—internal and external. HPL is a non-porous barrier that mold cannot penetrate, ensuring your valuables remain in a climate-stable environment.

Material Safety

Zero-HCHO (ENF Grade): Our plywood core meets the highest global safety standards for zero chemical off-gassing, engineered specifically for Brunei's tropical indoor air quality.
