Location: RPN Kampong Rimba

The "Narrow-Terrace" Optimization

Space: 120 sq. ft. Wet Kitchen

Timeline: 4 Weeks

Key Tech: Blum Space Tower

The Challenge: Linear Constraints

Standard National Housing Scheme (RPN) terrace houses in Rimba often feature long, narrow "galley" style kitchens. Our client, a family of five, struggled with a lack of preparation space and "dead zones" in deep corner cabinets where pots vanished, never to be seen again. The challenge was to increase storage volume by 30% without narrowing the walkway.

The Technical Solution

We moved away from traditional swing-door cabinets which block walkways.

  • Full-Extension Runners: We utilized heavy-duty runners capable of supporting 50kg loads. This allowed us to build deep drawers that extend fully, bringing the back of the cabinet to the user.
  • Vertical Pantry Integration: Implementing a "Space Tower" concept, we utilized the high ceilings common in Rimba homes to stack storage vertically, using internal pull-outs for dry food goods.
  • Corner Optimization: The "dead corner" was resolved using a specialized LeMans pull-out system, maximizing the blind corner space.

Design Insight

Optimizing standard RPN layouts requires specific knowledge of the structural constraints. Vertical storage is the most effective way to gain space without expanding the footprint of the home.

Location: Kota Batu / Riverfront

The Zero-HCHO (ENF) Humidity Protocol

Environment: High Humidity (85%+)

Safety: Zero-HCHO (ENF Grade)

Material: ENF Grade Plywood

The Challenge: The Tropical Climate

A residence near the Brunei River in Kota Batu faced a recurring issue: standard chipboard cabinets were swelling and delaminating due to the high ambient humidity and salt air. Furthermore, the client was concerned about the sharp, chemical smell often associated with new carpentry. They needed a solution that was durable, humidity-proof, and safe for their family.

The Technical Solution

We deployed our strict "Zero-HCHO (ENF Grade)" protocol for this installation. We do not use HMR board or standard chipboard.

  • ENF Grade Plywood Carcass: The core of every cabinet was constructed using ENF (Emission Non-Formaldehyde) Grade Plywoodâ€”the highest global standard for safety. Unlike standard boards which rely on toxic glues, our plywood offers structural integrity with "Zero Toxic" emissions.
  • Quartz Stone Surfaces: We installed non-porous Quartz stone countertops. Unlike natural granite, which has microscopic pores that can trap moisture and bacteria (leading to mold in humid climates), Quartz is sealed and impervious.
  • PVC Edging: All exposed board edges were machine-sealed with 1mm high-impact PVC edging using high-temperature adhesive to prevent moisture ingress at the seams.

Technical Note

This project demonstrates that health and durability go hand-in-hand. By using ENF Grade Plywood, we eliminated the risk of formaldehyde off-gassing while providing a material that naturally withstands the Brunei river climate better than any compressed chipboard.

Location: Jerudong Park Area

The "Social Island" Concept

Type: Dry Kitchen / Entertainment

Feature: Waterfall Island

Tech: Push-to-Open (Handleless)

The Challenge: Merging Function with Form

In modern Bruneian luxury homes, the "Dry Kitchen" is the heart of the homeâ€”a place for family gathering and serving guests, separate from the heavy cooking of the "Wet Kitchen." This client in Jerudong requested a centerpiece that felt more like furniture than cabinetry.

The Technical Solution

We focused on seamless integration and "invisible" hardware.

  • Waterfall Quartz: The island features a mitered waterfall edge where the stone flows down the sides, created with precision 45-degree cuts for a seamless vein match.
  • Handleless Systems: We utilized Blum TIP-ON mechanical opening support systems. A light touch opens the heavy drawers, eliminating the visual clutter of handles and ensuring a sleek, modern silhouette.
  • Integrated Lighting: Recessed LED profile lighting was routed into the cabinetry carcass to provide task lighting without visible fixtures.

Frequently Solved Problems

Why do you use ENF Plywood instead of HMR/Chipboard?

We prioritize your health and the longevity of your home. We do not use HMR or Chipboard. Instead, we exclusively use ENF Grade Plywood, which meets the "Zero-HCHO (ENF Grade)" standard. It is safer for your family and offers far superior structural strength against humidity compared to compressed particle boards.

Can you service Kuala Belait and Seria?

Yes. While our workshop is central, a significant portion of our "Project Chronicles" originate from the Belait district. We have specialized logistics to transport pre-assembled carcasses to KB for efficient on-site installation.

What is the best countertop for heavy cooking?

For heavy cooking (tumis/frying), we recommend Quartz Stone or Sintered Stone. Unlike solid surface (acrylic), Quartz is scratch-resistant and heat-resistant, making it ideal for the active Bruneian wet kitchen.

Do you provide 3D designs before building?

Yes. Part of our technical process involves detailed 3D modeling to visualize spatial flow and finish combinations before a single board is cut. This ensures the technical specifications meet your aesthetic vision.
