Brunei vs Miri Kitchen Cabinets

Many Bruneians consider buying kitchen cabinets from Miri workshops. Here's an honest comparison of cost, quality, and practicality to help you decide.

Side-by-Side Comparison

Factor Brunei-Made (Local) Miri-Made (Imported)
Quote price BND 7,000–18,000 (L-shape to U-shape) May appear 15–30% lower on paper
Transport cost Included (local delivery) BND 300–800 (single kitchen), BND 800–1,500 (full house)
Customs duty None ~5% of CIF value at Brunei border
On-site measurement Yes — laser measurement before quoting Usually remote (from photos or your own measurements)
Measurement accuracy Maker measures, maker responsible for fit You measure, you're responsible for errors
Humidity engineering Designed for 80–90% RH (Brunei conditions) Built to Malaysian standards (typically 60–75% RH)
Carcass material 18mm ENF-grade plywood Varies — often 16mm standard particleboard
Edge sealing 1mm ABS with EVA hot-melt at 190°C Varies — check if heat-sealed or cold press
Warranty Local — same team, same week Cross-border — you arrange return or local handyman
After-sales repair Phone call, local visit Transport back to Miri, or find matching materials locally
Lead time 10–14 weeks from confirmation 6–12 weeks fabrication + border crossing logistics
Installation In-house team, not subcontracted Miri team travels to Brunei (extra cost) or local subcontractor
Design flexibility 3D renders, unlimited revisions before fabrication Depends on workshop — often limited to their standard designs
Currency risk BND pricing, no exchange rate exposure MYR pricing — BND/MYR rate fluctuates
Border delays None Sungai Tujoh/Kuala Lurah crossing queues, customs inspection, possible delays

The Hidden Costs of Miri Cabinets

When comparing quotes, many homeowners look at the workshop price alone. Here's what gets added on top for a Miri order:

Cost Item Typical Range (BND)
Lorry transport (Miri → Brunei) 300–800 (kitchen only), 800–1,500 (full house)
Customs duty (~5% CIF) 350–900
Installation team travel (if Miri team comes) 200–500
Measurement error rework (if dimensions are off) 500–2,000+
Future warranty repair trip Unpredictable
Total hidden costs BND 1,350–4,700+

These costs often eliminate or reverse the initial price advantage of a Miri quote.

The Humidity Factor

This is the most overlooked risk when importing cabinets from Miri into Brunei:

Brunei Climate

  • 80–90% average relative humidity
  • Consistent year-round (equatorial)
  • Wet kitchens face direct water + steam daily
  • Cabinets must be engineered for extreme moisture

Interior Sarawak (Miri)

  • 60–75% average relative humidity
  • Seasonal variation
  • Standard materials often sufficient
  • Cabinets built to this lower standard may fail in Brunei

Cabinets built for 60–75% RH installed in an 80–90% RH environment can develop: edge banding peeling, carcass swelling, mould growth behind panels, and drawer runner corrosion — typically within 1–3 years.

When Miri Does Make Sense

We're being honest — there are scenarios where Miri is a reasonable choice:

  • Budget-first projects — If total cost is the only priority and you accept the warranty and humidity trade-offs
  • Specific Malaysian brands — If you want a specific brand or design catalogue only available through Malaysian distributors
  • Temporary or rental properties — If cabinets only need to last 3–5 years, humidity engineering matters less
  • Dry-only rooms — Wardrobes and TV consoles in air-conditioned bedrooms face less humidity stress than wet kitchens

For permanent homes and wet kitchen areas, the humidity engineering difference is significant enough to justify local fabrication.

Questions to Ask Any Miri Workshop

If you do decide to get a Miri quote, ask these questions to make a fair comparison:

  1. Is the quote workshop price only, or does it include transport to Brunei and installation?
  2. What carcass board do you use? Is it moisture-resistant? What grade (E1, E0, ENF)?
  3. How is edge banding applied — heat-sealed (EVA/PUR) or cold press?
  4. Who measures the space — do you come to Brunei, or do I provide measurements?
  5. What happens if the cabinets don't fit due to measurement error?
  6. What is the warranty? Does it cover cross-border service in Brunei?
  7. Who handles installation in Brunei? Your team or a subcontractor?
  8. Is the price in MYR or BND? What exchange rate do you use?
  9. What is the total customs duty I should expect at the Brunei border?

What About Taobao & Chinese Online Platforms?

Some Bruneians skip Miri entirely and order cabinets from Taobao or Shopee China. The risks are even greater: shipping costs BND 2,000–5,000+, no on-site measurement, materials built for 30–50% RH (even drier than Miri), and zero local after-sales. The total landed cost often matches local pricing — but without the warranty or fit.
