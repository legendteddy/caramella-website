The Real Question

Don’t Compare Price. Compare Cost Per Year.

The lowest-cost kitchen is rarely the best deal. What matters is how much your cabinets cost you per year of use — because a kitchen that lasts 5 years at BND 3,000 costs more than one that lasts 20 years at BND 8,000.

Why “Lower-Cost” Can Become Expensive

Budget cabinets from Miri, Taobao, or flat-pack imports fail in Brunei’s 80–90% humidity within 3–5 years. When they do, you pay again — not just for new cabinets, but for demolition, disposal, plumbing disconnection, countertop removal, and reinstallation. Each replacement cycle costs more than just the cabinets.

Cabinetry built for Brunei’s climate uses humidity-engineered materials that don’t swell, edge banding that doesn’t peel, and hardware that doesn’t corrode. The result: you pay once and use it for decades.

What to Ask When Comparing Proposals

Instead of “which proposal is lowest-cost?”, ask:

  1. What board do you use, and is it ENF-certified? Caramella uses ENF-grade plywood (<0.010 mg/m³ formaldehyde — lab verified). If they can’t answer, that’s your answer.
  2. What’s the edge banding method? Cold-press fails in 1–2 years. EVA hot-melt or PUR lasts decades.
  3. What hardware brand? Blum, Hettich, or branded runners with coating — not “soft-close” without a name.
  4. What’s the realistic lifespan in a Brunei wet kitchen? If they hesitate, they know it won’t last.
  5. What does your warranty cover? Our standard is a 1-year universal hardware warranty backed by our local team. Be wary of 'lifetime' claims that are difficult to claim locally.

Comparing with Miri or Taobao Prices?

Many Bruneians compare our prices with Miri workshops or Taobao listings. Miri proposals may appear 15–30% lower, but the total landed cost often tells a different story once you add transport (BND 300–800), customs duty (~5%), and the lack of local support.

We recommend getting our proposal first — with free on-site laser measurement — then comparing the total cost fairly.
