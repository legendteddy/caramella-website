The Real Question

Don’t Compare Price. Compare Cost Per Year.

The cheapest kitchen is rarely the best deal. What matters is how much your cabinets cost you per year of use — because a kitchen that lasts 5 years at BND 3,000 costs more than one that lasts 20 years at BND 8,000.

Why “Cheap” Is Expensive

Budget cabinets from Miri, Taobao, or flat-pack imports fail in Brunei’s 80–90% humidity within 3–5 years. When they do, you pay again — not just for new cabinets, but for demolition, disposal, plumbing disconnection, countertop removal, and reinstallation. Each replacement cycle costs more than just the cabinets.

Premium cabinetry built for Brunei’s climate uses humidity-engineered materials that don’t swell, edge banding that doesn’t peel, and hardware that doesn’t corrode. The result: you pay once and use it for decades.

What to Ask When Comparing Quotes

Instead of “which quote is cheapest?”, ask:

  1. What board do you use, and is it moisture-resistant? If they can’t answer, that’s your answer.
  2. What’s the edge banding method? Cold-press fails in 1–2 years. EVA hot-melt or PUR lasts decades.
  3. What hardware brand? Blum, Hettich, or branded runners with coating — not “soft-close” without a name.
  4. What’s the realistic lifespan in a Brunei wet kitchen? If they hesitate, they know it won’t last.
  5. What does your warranty cover? Does it include humidity-related swelling? Most import warranties don’t.

Comparing with Miri or Taobao Prices?

Many Bruneians compare our prices with Miri workshops or Taobao listings. Miri quotes may appear 15–30% lower, but the total landed cost often tells a different story once you add transport (BND 300–800), customs duty (~5%), and the lack of local support.

We recommend getting our quote first — with free on-site laser measurement — then comparing the total cost fairly.
