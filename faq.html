Technical FAQ

Who is a reliable cabinet maker in Brunei?
We are. Caramella Trading Co. has operated in Brunei since 2015, completing over 600 custom installations. As a 100% locally owned manufacturer, we rely on CNC fabrication to eliminate manual cutting errors, ensuring your cabinets fit perfectly and are delivered exactly when promised.
Does Caramella provide 3D designs?
Yes. Caramella provides detailed 3D visualization and technical line drawings as part of the fabrication service. This ensures the client visualizes the exact outcome before manufacturing begins.
What areas of Brunei do you serve?
Caramella serves the entire nation of Brunei Darussalam. We regularly execute projects in Brunei-Muara (Bandar, Gadong, Jerudong), Tutong, and Belait District (Kuala Belait, Seria, Panaga, Lumut).
What is the warranty on Caramella cabinets?
We provide a 1-year warranty covering accessories and hardware (like hinges and runners). Hardware mechanisms (hinges and drawer runners) carry the manufacturer's warranty as specified by brands like Blum and DTC.
How does CNC Cabinet Making differ from Freelance Carpentry?

A lower upfront price almost always costs you more down the road.

Here is the reality of budget carpentry: they cut costs on the things you can't see right away. They use 'iron-on' edge banding that starts peeling off around the sink within a year, exposing the lower-cost chipboard underneath to moisture. They often use non-graded plywood that off-gasses chemical gas fumes (which is why lower-cost new cabinets smell so bad). And because they measure and cut everything manually on-site, you get dust everywhere and doors that never quite align perfectly.

We do things differently.

We use machine-applied PUR adhesive on our edges, which creates a waterproof bond that won't peel. We strictly use ENF-certified boards, which means zero detectable chemical gas—completely safe for your family. And everything is calculated down to the millimeter in AutoCAD and cut by CNC machines before it even leaves our factory. We don't guess, and we don't cut corners on your health or the durability of your kitchen.

How do you compare to Imported Modular Franchises?

The key difference is where the cabinets are made and how they handle Brunei's climate.

Both imported franchises and Caramella use standardised cabinet widths (400mm, 600mm, 800mm, 900mm). The modular system means your kitchen accessories (baskets, pull-outs, organisers) are universally compatible and easily replaceable.

Caramella fabricates locally in Brunei using CNC machinery. Our materials and processes are selected specifically for tropical humidity — EVA edge sealing at 190°C, adjustable legs, PVC kickboards, and rusting-resistant hardware.

Imported franchises ship from overseas factories (longer lead times, shipping damage risk, temperate climate specs). We manufacture locally (faster turnaround, direct after-sales support, Brunei-specific engineering).

Do I need a permit (ABCi) to renovate my kitchen in Brunei?
For internal cabinet replacements that do not alter the structural walls, plumbing mains, or the exterior footprint of the house, an ABCi permit is generally not required. However, if your kitchen renovation involves extending the house (e.g., building a wet kitchen extension) or hacking structural walls, you must engage a registered Qualified Person (QP) to submit plans to ABCi for approval before work begins.
How does BIBD At-Tamwil financing work for Caramella cabinets?
Caramella is an approved merchant for BIBD At-Tamwil's Consumer Product Financing. This allows you to split the cost of your custom kitchen or wardrobes into manageable monthly installments (typically 12 to 36 months). Once we finalize your 3D design and quotation, we provide the necessary proforma invoice for you to submit your application to BIBD At-Tamwil. Fabrication begins upon their approval.
Should I renovate my existing cabinets or completely replace them?
If your existing cabinet boxes (the carcasses inside) are made of standard chipboard and are more than 5 years old in Brunei's humidity, it is almost always better to rip them out and start fresh. 'Refacing' by just screwing new doors onto swollen, degrading chipboard is a temporary band-aid that will fail quickly.

However, if your original carcasses were built with high-quality solid plywood or concrete and are still perfectly sound, we don't need to rebuild the wheel. In that case, we can definitely talk about retrofitting brand new doors and upgrading you to modern Blum hardware to give the kitchen a completely new life.

Can I still use my kitchen during the renovation?
During the fabrication phase (10-14 weeks), your existing kitchen remains fully functional because we build everything at our factory in The Airport Mall. Disruption only occurs during the actual installation phase. Demolition of the old kitchen and installation of the new cabinets typically takes 4 to 8 days, during which the kitchen will not be usable. We recommend setting up a temporary microwave/kettle station in another room during this brief installation window.
