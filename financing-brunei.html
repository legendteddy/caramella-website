Frequently Asked Questions

What is BIBD At-Tamwil?

BIBD At-Tamwil is a Shariah-compliant financing facility offered by Bank Islam Brunei Darussalam (BIBD). It allows you to purchase goods or services from approved vendors and pay in installments.

Is Caramella an approved vendor?

Yes. Caramella Trading Company is listed as an approved BIBD At-Tamwil vendor for kitchen and wardrobe purchases.

What's the minimum and maximum I can finance?

BIBD At-Tamwil typically covers financing from BND 2,000 to BND 50,000. The exact limit depends on your eligibility and project scope.

What documents do I need?

Common requirements include: Brunei IC, latest salary slips (3 months), bank statements, and the quotation from Caramella. BIBD may request additional documents based on your application.

How long does approval take?

Typically 3-5 business days, depending on document completeness and BIBD's review workload.

Can I pay early without penalty?

BIBD At-Tamwil generally allows early settlement. Check your financing agreement for specific terms or contact BIBD directly.

Does financing change the quality or timeline?

No. Whether you pay upfront or finance, you receive the same materials, same CNC precision, and same service standards. Timeline depends on project scope, not payment method.

Can I combine financing with a discount or promotion?

We don't run artificial discounts. Our pricing is consistent year-round. Financing terms are set by BIBD, not Caramella.

Compare Financing Options Before You Commit

Different vendors have different quality standards. When comparing quotes, ask:

  • What edge sealing method do you use? (EVA at 180°C–190°C is the standard for moisture resistance)
  • What drawer slides? (DTC side-mount or Blum TANDEM concealed?)
  • How are drawer boxes constructed? (18mm boards with pocket screws, or thinner?)

These 3 questions reveal build quality. Financing the wrong cabinet is still a mistake — make sure you're comparing properly.
