Kitchen Countertop Materials in Brunei, Compared

Countertop material affects durability, maintenance, heat resistance, and cost. Here is how the main options perform in Brunei's climate.

Last updated: 24 February 2026

Quick Answer

Who this page is for: Homeowners choosing between premium Quartz and budget-friendly Formica in a tropical environment.

Use this page to compare materials on equal terms, then discuss your specific needs with any cabinet maker.

Material Comparison

The four most common countertop materials available in Brunei each have distinct performance characteristics:

Property Quartz Composite Formica
Composition 93% ground quartz + 7% resin binder High-pressure decorative formica bonded to particleboard
Heat resistance Moderate — use a mat for hot pans. Surface can discolour above 150°C. Low — burns or peels from direct heat.
Scratch resistance High — Mohs hardness 7 Moderate — resistant to light scratches, but deep scratches expose core material
Stain resistance High — non-porous, no sealing required Good — non-porous surface resists most stains
Repairability Difficult — chips require professional repair or replacement Poor — damaged sections must be replaced
Seam visibility Visible but minimal with proper joining Visible at joints with exposed edges
Price range (Brunei) BND 180–350 per linear foot installed BND 30–80 per linear foot installed
Best suited for Primary dry kitchens, islands, peninsula counters Budget builds, rental properties, utility areas

Climate Considerations for Brunei

Brunei's high humidity (80–90% year-round) has specific implications for countertop selection:

  • Quartz handles humidity well: Liquid-proof engineered surface — no sealing required. Humidity does not affect the material. This is the most maintenance-free option for tropical climates.
  • Formica edge exposure: The formica surface is waterproof, but the particleboard core material is not. In wet kitchen environments, ensure all edges are fully sealed and that the formica-to-core material bond uses waterproof adhesive.

Wet Kitchen vs Dry Kitchen Recommendations

In Brunei, many homes have separate wet and dry kitchens. Material recommendations:

  • Dry kitchen (primary): Quartz composite is the superior choice for primary kitchens — liquid-proof, zero maintenance, wide colour range, and consistent appearance.
  • Wet kitchen: Quartz composite is recommended for its high resistance to heavy frying, turmeric stains, and heat. For utility areas, Formica is a cost-effective alternative if edges are properly sealed.

What to Ask When Comparing Quotes

  • What brand and grade of quartz is specified?
  • What is the slab thickness — 20mm or 30mm?
  • Is the countertop price per linear foot or per square foot?
  • Are edge profiles included in the price, or charged extra?
  • Is undermount sink cutout and polishing included?
