Hardware Durability Benchmark in High-Humidity Brunei Conditions

Executive Summary

This protocol benchmarks hinge and runner durability with cycle and humidity stress criteria relevant to Brunei use conditions.

Claim Labeling Rules

  • `Measured`: cycle-test and field-return observations.
  • `Cited`: vendor technical and standards references.
  • `Inference`: benchmark score derived from weighted criteria.

Research Question

Which hardware models retain function longest under high cycle counts and high humidity exposure?

Methodology

  1. Define standardized test profile (load, cycle rate, humidity band).
  2. Run bench tests and collect field-return corroboration.
  3. Score failure modes and maintenance burden.
  4. Publish weighted ranking with transparent rubric.

Primary Endpoints

  • cycles_to_failure
  • corrosion_grade
  • load_retention_percent
  • benchmark_score_100

Assumptions

  • Bench profile approximates high-use residential operation.
  • Field-return records are consistently coded by failure class.

Limitations

  • Brand/model availability may change during study.
  • Warranty terms do not directly equal durability performance.

Independent Validation Status

Protocol complete, measured dataset pending.

What This Does Not Prove

  • Universal ranking for all untested models.
  • Performance under misuse conditions outside test profile.
