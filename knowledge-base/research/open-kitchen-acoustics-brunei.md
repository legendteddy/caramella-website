# Acoustics in Open Kitchens in Brunei Homes

## Executive Summary
This protocol measures noise from hood and appliance combinations in open-plan kitchens and translates results into comfort-grade design thresholds.

## Claim Labeling Rules
- `Measured`: dBA measurements and occupant ratings.
- `Cited`: standards and technical guidance.
- `Inference`: comfort band mapping from measured values.

## Research Question
Which appliance and layout combinations maintain acceptable perceived comfort in open kitchens?

## Methodology
1. Measure dBA at defined listener points and operating scenarios.
2. Record room geometry and surface characteristics.
3. Collect occupant comfort scores after each scenario.
4. Model combined noise impact and recommend target bands.

## Primary Endpoints
- combined_dba
- speech_interference_flag
- occupant_comfort_score_10
- mitigation_delta_dba

## Assumptions
- Measurement points represent real conversation zones.
- Comfort scoring scale is consistently applied.

## Limitations
- Subjective comfort varies across households.
- Furnishing changes can alter acoustic behavior.

## Independent Validation Status
Protocol complete — field data collection active, March 2026.

## What This Does Not Prove
- Universal comfort for every user profile.
- Studio-grade acoustic performance.
