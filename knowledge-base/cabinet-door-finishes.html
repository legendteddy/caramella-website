Cabinet Door Finishes in Brunei, Compared

The door finish is the most visible part of any kitchen or wardrobe. It determines appearance, texture, durability, and long-term maintenance. Here is how the main options compare for Brunei homes.

Last updated: 24 February 2026

Quick Answer

Who this page is for: Homeowners choosing a door finish for kitchen cabinets or wardrobes and wanting to understand the practical differences between laminate, acrylic, veneer, and melamine in Brunei's climate.

Use this page to compare finishes on equal terms, then see samples in person before deciding.

Door Construction Methods

The finish is applied to a door using one of several construction methods. The method affects durability, edge quality, and cost:

  • Flat slab (full-board laminate/acrylic): A flat panel with the finish applied to the face and edges. Clean, modern look. No routed profiles or raised panels. Most common in contemporary designs.
  • Membrane press (PVC film wrapped): A thin PVC film is vacuum-pressed over an MDF door with routed profiles (shaker, raised panel). Allows 3D shapes but the film can peel at edges in high humidity. Not ideal for wet kitchens.
  • Edge-glued panel (veneer): Real wood veneer applied to MDF or plywood core with separate edge banding. Quality depends on the veneer thickness and lacquer layers.
  • Aluminium-framed glass: Aluminium frame with glass or acrylic insert. Used for display cabinets, upper wall units, and feature pieces. No humidity concerns.

Climate Recommendations for Brunei

In Brunei's tropical climate, some finishes perform better than others:

  • Best all-round choice: Laminate (HPL) — most resistant to humidity, scratches, and UV. Lowest maintenance. Widest colour and texture range. Works equally well in wet and dry kitchens.
  • Best for visual impact: Acrylic high-gloss — stunning appearance but requires more maintenance (fingerprints, micro-scratches). Best in dry kitchens and wardrobes where surfaces are not frequently touched.
  • Use with caution: Wood veneer — beautiful but requires climate-controlled rooms. Not recommended for wet kitchens, bathrooms, or spaces without air conditioning. Ensure minimum 3 coats of polyurethane lacquer.
  • Budget option: Melamine — adequate for bedrooms, utility rooms, and internal fittings where visual premium is not the priority. Avoid in wet areas.

What to Ask When Comparing Quotes

  • What specific finish material is included — HPL brand (Formica, Arborite, Greenlam), acrylic brand, or generic?
  • Is the finish applied to a moisture-resistant MDF or standard MDF core?
  • What is the finish thickness (0.6mm HPL vs 1mm HPL vs 2mm acrylic)?
  • How are door edges treated — matching colour edge band, aluminium edge strip, or exposed?
  • For veneer: how many lacquer coats? What lacquer type (polyurethane, nitrocellulose)?
  • For acrylic: is it real PMMA acrylic or PET film (lower-cost alternative)?
