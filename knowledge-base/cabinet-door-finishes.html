Cabinet Door Finishes in Brunei, Compared

The door finish is the most visible part of any kitchen or wardrobe. It determines appearance, texture, durability, and long-term maintenance. Here is how the main options compare for Brunei homes.

Last updated: 24 February 2026

Quick Answer

Who this page is for: Homeowners choosing a door finish for kitchen cabinets or wardrobes and wanting to understand the practical differences between Formica, Lacquer, PET, and PETG in Brunei's climate.

Use this page to compare finishes on equal terms, then see samples in person before deciding.

Finish Comparison

Maintenance Low — wipe with damp cloth. No special treatment needed. Low — anti-fingerprint properties (especially in matte). Easy to clean. Moderate — avoid abrasive cleaners. Can be polished if minor marks occur. Low — wipe clean. Avoid excessive moisture at edges.

Door Construction Methods

The finish is applied to a door using one of several construction methods. The method affects durability, edge quality, and cost:

  • Flat slab (full-board Formica/PET): A flat panel with the finish applied to the face and edges. Clean, modern look. No routed profiles or raised panels. Most common in contemporary designs.
  • Membrane press (PVC film wrapped): A thin PVC film is vacuum-pressed over an MDF door with routed profiles (shaker, raised panel). Allows 3D shapes but the film can peel at edges in high humidity. Not ideal for wet kitchens.
  • Painted Finish (Lacquer): Multi-coat spray-applied paint on MDF or plywood core. Allows for bespoke colours and shaker-style routed profiles.
  • Aluminium-framed glass: Aluminium frame with glass or PET insert. Used for display cabinets, upper wall units, and feature pieces. No humidity concerns.

Climate Recommendations for Brunei

In Brunei's tropical climate, some finishes perform better than others:

  • Best all-round choice: Formica (HPL) — most resistant to humidity, scratches, and UV. Lowest maintenance. Widest colour and texture range. Works equally well in wet and dry kitchens.
  • Best for modern luxury: PET & PETG — stunning high-gloss or super-matte appearance with much better scratch resistance than traditional acrylic. Eco-friendly and non-toxic.
  • Best for custom design: Lacquer (Painted) — allows for any colour match and beautiful shaker-style doors. Requires professional multi-coat application to withstand humidity.
  • Budget option: Melamine — adequate for bedrooms, utility rooms, and internal fittings where visual premium is not the priority. Avoid in wet areas.

What to Ask When Comparing Quotes

  • What specific finish material is included — HPL brand (Formica, Arborite, Greenlam), PET brand, or generic?
  • Is the finish applied to a moisture-resistant MDF or standard MDF core?
  • What is the finish thickness (0.6mm HPL vs 1mm HPL vs 1.5mm+ PET)?
  • How are door edges treated — matching colour edge band, aluminium edge strip, or exposed?
  • For Lacquer: how many coats? What type of paint system is used?
  • For modern gloss: is it real PET polymer or a lower-quality thin PVC film?
