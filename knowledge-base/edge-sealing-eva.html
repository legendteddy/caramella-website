Edge Sealing in Brunei: Why It Matters

The fastest way cabinets fail in humid climates is at the edges. If you only ask one technical question, ask this.

Last updated: 24 February 2026

Quick Answer

Who this page is for: Homeowners comparing cabinet quotes and checking whether the edge sealing method is suitable for Brunei humidity.

Use this page to ask the right technical questions, then compare the full specification on the Build Standard page.

Why Edge Sealing Is the First Thing to Check

In Brunei's 80–90% relative humidity, the exposed edges of particleboard panels are the most vulnerable point in any cabinet. An unsealed or poorly sealed edge allows moisture to penetrate the substrate, causing swelling that starts at 2–3% and can reach 8–12% thickness increase within months. Once swelling begins, it is irreversible — the board cannot return to its original dimensions.

Edge sealing is the simplest, most cost-effective defense against humidity damage. A 1mm ABS edge band applied with hot-melt adhesive at 190°C creates a waterproof barrier that prevents moisture from entering through the panel edges. The quality of this seal — how it is applied, what adhesive is used, and how thick the edge band is — varies enormously between cabinet makers.

EVA vs PUR Adhesive

The two main adhesive types used in edge banding are EVA (Ethylene Vinyl Acetate) and PUR (Polyurethane Reactive). Both are heat-activated, but they behave differently:

Property EVA PUR
Application temperature 180–210°C 120–150°C
Bond type Thermoplastic — re-softens when reheated Reactive — cross-links with moisture, creating a permanent bond
Moisture resistance Good for most residential applications in climate-controlled rooms Superior — once cured, the bond is waterproof and cannot be re-softened by heat
Cost Lower — most common in residential cabinetry Higher — more common in laboratory, hospital, and marine applications
Bond line visibility Thin glue line, typically yellow or transparent Nearly invisible glue line
Suitable for Brunei? Yes — adequate for most residential kitchens when machine-applied at correct temperature Yes — preferred for wet kitchens, bathrooms, laundry rooms, and high-splash zones

For most residential kitchens in Brunei, properly applied EVA edge banding provides adequate moisture protection. PUR is the better choice for areas with direct water exposure (wet kitchens, bathroom vanities, laundry cabinets).

Machine Application vs Manual

How edge banding is applied matters as much as what adhesive is used:

  • Automatic edge banding machine: Applies adhesive at consistent temperature (190°C±5°C) and pressure across the entire edge. The machine trims excess edge band flush with the panel surface. This produces a uniform, sealed bond with no gaps or air pockets. Standard practice for quality cabinet workshops.
  • Manual iron-on application: An iron or heat gun is used to activate pre-glued edge tape by hand. Temperature and pressure vary along the length. Gaps and air pockets are common, especially at corners and ends. The seal is less consistent and may fail earlier in humid environments. Typically used in small DIY shops or for on-site repairs.
  • Cold press / contact adhesive: Edge band is stuck on using contact cement at room temperature. No heat activation. Weakest bond method. Not recommended for high-humidity environments.

Edge Band Thickness

Edge banding comes in different thicknesses, each with trade-offs:

  • 0.4mm PVC/ABS: Thinnest option. Low cost. Adequate for internal edges that are not exposed to moisture or cleaning. Can chip or peel at corners over time.
  • 1mm ABS: Standard for quality residential cabinetry. Provides a visible, durable edge that resists impact and moisture. This is the minimum recommended thickness for exposed edges in Brunei homes.
  • 2mm ABS/Acrylic: Premium option for visible edges on islands, peninsula units, and feature pieces. Provides a substantial feel and is nearly indistinguishable from a solid panel edge. Machine-trimming and buffing required for a seamless finish.

How to Inspect Edge Sealing Quality

You can visually and physically check edge banding quality on completed cabinets or showroom samples:

  • Run your fingernail along the edge: The transition from panel surface to edge band should be smooth, with no lip or gap. If you can catch your fingernail, the trim is not flush.
  • Check the corners: Corners are where manual application typically fails. Look for lifting, bubbling, or gaps at 90° transitions.
  • Look at the glue line: A thin, consistent glue line is normal. Thick, uneven, or yellow-brown glue squeeze-out suggests excess adhesive was applied.
  • Press firmly on the edge: Properly bonded edge banding should not flex or move. If it gives under thumb pressure, the bond may be weak.

What to Ask When Comparing Quotes

  • Is edging applied by automatic machine or manually (iron-on)?
  • What adhesive type is used — EVA, PUR, or cold press?
  • What is the edge band thickness — 0.4mm, 1mm, or 2mm?
  • What material — PVC or ABS? (ABS is more environmentally friendly and impact-resistant.)
  • How are sink areas and wet zone edges treated — same or different specification?
